04 — Foundation
Motion
Animations are calm and considered — never flashy. They guide attention, not demand it. Every transition should feel like turning a page, not a loading spinner.
Fade Up (Scroll)
0.7s ease
translateY(28px → 0)
opacity(0 → 1)
Section entrances, card reveals on scroll. Stagger siblings by 100ms.
Hover Lift
0.2s ease
translateY(0 → -2px)
Buttons, cards. Subtle — 2px max. Never bounce or spring.
Opacity Fade
0.2s ease
opacity(1 → 0.85)
Button hover state. Cleaner than color change on dark bg.
Nav Scroll
0.3s ease
box-shadow appears
Nav shadow on scroll past 40px. Signals depth, not movement.
Hero Load
0.7s ease
80ms base + 100ms stagger
On load: kicker → headline → sub → CTA → proof. Sequential, never simultaneous.
Forbidden
bounce / spring
rotate / flip
No playful or elastic easings. No spinning loaders. Calm at all times.
05 — Brand
Voice & Tone
fitted. speaks like a brilliant mentor who respects your time. It never sells. It never cheerleads. It informs, guides, and — when necessary — is candid.
fitted. is
Trusted. Speaks from evidence, not enthusiasm. States facts confidently.
Calm. Never urgent. No countdown timers, no "spots filling fast."
Direct. Says the thing. Doesn't pad, hedge, or use filler phrases.
Intelligent. Assumes the reader is smart. Never over-explains.
Candid. Will say what's not working — the way a good mentor would.
Warm. Empathy without sentimentality. Sees the person behind the resume.
fitted. is not
Hustle-culture. No "crush it," "land your dream job," "level up."
Salesy. No exclamation points. No LIMITED TIME OFFER.
Corporate. No buzzwords: synergy, leverage, pivot, disrupt.
Hollow. No "we believe in you!" without specific insight behind it.
Frantic. Never uses urgency as manipulation. Never creates FOMO.
Vague. Every claim is specific. "94% match" not "great fit."
Copy Examples — Headlines what good looks like
✓ Use this
"Your search, guided by real intelligence."
"Know exactly where you stand — before you apply."
"A better search starts today."
Calm. Specific. Adult. Uses Garamond italic for a single word of weight.
✗ Avoid this
"Supercharge your job search with AI!"
"Land your dream job today 🚀"
"The #1 platform for career success!"
Excitable. Vague. Juvenile. Emoji. Hustle-coded.

09 — Components
Icon Style
Icons are structural, not decorative. Use sparingly. The fitted. icon style is stroke-based, 1.4–1.5px stroke weight, rounded caps, geometric but not playful.

Icon rules
Size: 18–24px in UI. Never smaller than 16px. Color: Navy (#2f3e5c) on light backgrounds. Off-white on dark. Taupe for secondary/dimmed state. Style: Stroke only — no fills, no solid icons. Avoid: emoji-style icons, filled shapes, vivid colors, decorative icons that add no meaning.

Natural, directional light (window light ideal)

Diverse representation across age, identity

✗ Photography Don'ts
Stock-photo smiling at a blank screen
Overly bright, washed-out office environments
Fist pumps, celebrations, trophy poses
Ultra-saturated or HDR processing
White cube / studio backgrounds

Color treatment on photos
All photos should be slightly desaturated (−10 to −20% saturation) with a subtle warm-taupe grade. Avoid cool blue tones. If overlaying text, use a navy overlay at 40–60% opacity or a warm blur treatment.

Social content rules
Typography on social EB Garamond for headline. DM Sans for labels and captions. Always left-align or center — never right-align text. Minimum 32px for any readable text in graphics.
Caption voice Short. Direct. No hashtag spam — max 3–5 relevant tags. No exclamation points. Write like you're texting a smart friend, not pitching a product.
12 — Guidelines
Do's & Don'ts
The most important rules for maintaining fitted.'s premium, quiet-luxury editorial standard across all touchpoints.
Visual Identity
✓ Do
✓
Use generous white space — let elements breathe. A crowded fitted. is not fitted.
✓
Lead with the Garamond serif for any headline or callout. It's what makes us recognizable.
✓
Keep backgrounds within the approved palette: navy, off-white (#f4f2ed), white (#faf9f6), taupe-pale.
✓
Use one italic word per headline for emphasis — not the whole sentence.
✓
Keep the taupe accent as warmth, not as a primary color. It supports navy; it doesn't replace it.
✓
Use horizontal rules and fine lines to separate, not borders or boxes.
✗ Don't
✗
Use gradients as background fills. The palette is flat, warm, and grounded — not gradient-washed.
✗
Introduce new typefaces. EB Garamond + DM Sans only. System fonts are not acceptable for designed assets.
✗
Use emoji in any designed asset or headline copy. They break the editorial tone immediately.
✗
Use vivid colors (bright blue, green, purple, red) for any UI element except explicit error states.
✗
Round corners excessively. Pill-shaped buttons or cards feel consumer-app, not professional platform.
✗
Add drop shadows for decoration. Shadows only appear when elevation is meaningful (e.g. cards floating over backgrounds).
Copy & Voice
✓ Do
✓
Write as if the reader is a 38-year-old senior PM who has zero patience for marketing speak.
✓
Use specific numbers and evidence: "94% match" not "great opportunity." Precision signals respect.
✓
Make AI insights candid even when uncomfortable. That's the product's core value proposition.
✓
End CTAs with something honest: "it's free" or "cancel anytime" — never "No credit card required!!" with exclamation.
✗ Don't
✗
Use exclamation points anywhere in UI, marketing, or social copy. Full stop.
✗
Create urgency artificially: "Only 3 spots left," "Offer expires soon," "Don't miss out."
✗
Use passive, hedging language: "We think you might potentially be a good fit, perhaps." Be clear.
✗
Write AI insights that are only positive. Honest means balanced. Cheerleading erodes trust.
Social Media Specific
✓ Do
✓
Use text-dominant graphics. The brand voice is the visual — a strong Garamond headline IS the asset.
✓
Stick to navy, off-white, and taupe-pale as graphic backgrounds. Maximum 2 colors per graphic.
✓
Keep hashtags topical (3–5 max): #careers #jobsearch #productmanagement etc.
✗ Don't
✗
Use Canva templates with stock decorations: confetti, arrows, stars, speech bubbles, neon accents.
✗
Put low-quality screenshots or app mockups in social graphics unless they're crisp and on-brand.
✗
Post motivational quotes with generic backgrounds: "Success is a journey, not a destination 🔥"
